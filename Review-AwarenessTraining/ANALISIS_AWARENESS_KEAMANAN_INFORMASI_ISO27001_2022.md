- ✓ **23 peserta (12.1%)** mencapai nilai excellent (85+)
- 📊 **Distribusi normal** menunjukkan efektivitas sosialisasi, namun perlu reinforcement

### 4.2 Topik-Topik dengan Gap Signifikan (Perlukan Fokus Improvement)

#### **🔴 CRITICAL GAP: Asset Classification & Confidentiality (Avg: 2.69/10)**
- **Issue:** Peserta kesulitan membedakan level klassifikasi aset (public, internal, confidential, restricted)
- **Root Cause:** 
  - Konsep abstract, butuh konteksualisasi pada real business scenario
  - Kurang practical examples dan templates
  - Policy belum consistent di organisasi
- **Impact:** Dapat menyebabkan over/under protection terhadap aset
- **Action Plan:**
  - Develop asset classification guideline yang sederhana dan visual
  - Create job aids dan checklists untuk setiap departemen
  - Implement regular asset review dengan owner
  - Develop user-friendly classification tool
  - Training fokus pada "why" bukan "what"

#### **🟠 SIGNIFICANT GAP: Intellectual Property Rights (Avg: 3.39/10)**
- **Issue:** Misunderstanding tentang ownership developer dan perusahaan
- **Root Cause:**
  - Legal nuance yang complex
  - Blurred boundary antara personal project dan corporate asset
  - Kurangnya clear policy dan agreement template
- **Impact:** Potential IP dispute, legal exposure, knowledge loss
- **Action Plan:**
  - Develop clear "work for hire" policy
  - Template IP agreement untuk contractor/external developer
  - Legal session khusus dengan tech team
  - Case study approach untuk better understanding

#### **🟠 SIGNIFICANT GAP: Control Assessment & Audit Finding (Avg: 3.71/10)**
- **Issue:** Peserta tidak understand cara assess control dan handle audit finding
- **Root Cause:**
  - Topic yang advanced, belum siap mayoritas peserta
  - Practical experience terbatas
  - Kurangnya hands-on dengan audit tools
- **Impact:** Audit responses tidak proper, remediation tidak effective
- **Action Plan:**
  - Develop risk & control matrix yang simple
  - Hands-on workshop dengan audit examples
  - Create control self-assessment tool
  - Regular practice assessment dengan feedback

#### **🟡 MODERATE GAP: Password Policy & Technical Control (Avg: 3.79/10)**
- **Issue:** Misconception tentang password complexity dan rotation policy
- **Root Cause:**
  - Berbagai industry best practice yang conflicting
  - Technical jargon yang kurang user-friendly
  - Kesulitan balance between security dan usability
- **Impact:** Weak password practice, security incident risk
- **Action Plan:**
  - Simplify password policy ke actionable dos dan don'ts
  - Visual guide dan demo tool
  - Implement password manager training
  - Regular awareness about common password mistakes

## V. KEY FINDINGS & INSIGHTS

### 5.1 Temuan Utama

1. **Awareness Gap berbanding terbalik dengan Departemen Technical Role**
   - Paradox: Operational & Finance (high transaction volume) memiliki awareness lebih rendah
   - Tech team (Digital Solution, Business Analyst) perform comparable atau better
   - Implication: Sosialisasi resonates lebih dengan analytical minds vs. operational mindset

2. **Compliance Department adalah Concern Utama**
   - Lowest performing department (avg 48) mendorong compliance/legal
   - Jika compliance staff tidak understand SMKI, hard to enforce across organization
   - Immediate action required untuk department ini

3. **Size doesn't guarantee understanding**
   - Largest department (Finance Accounting, 35 people) dengan average 75.6 - respectable
   - BUT Largest operational department (Operations, 32 people) dengan 62.4 - below average
   - Training effectiveness tergantung delivery method, bukan scale

4. **Asset & Risk Management adalah Blind Spot**
   - Peserta understand "apa itu ISO 27001" tapi tidak understand "bagaimana apply"
   - Gap antara theoretical knowledge dan practical implementation
   - Perlu translate control requirements ke departmental business language

5. **Executive Awareness Inconsistent**
   - CEO Office average 68.6, range sangat lebar (38-90)
   - Implication: Tidak semua leadership fully support security initiatives
   - Risk untuk executive sponsorship dan resource allocation

### 5.2 Positive Indicators 

- ✓ Awareness tentang regulatory requirements (UU PDP) sangat kuat
- ✓ Practical threat awareness (fraud, deepfake, incident) responsive
- ✓ BIA/BCM concept well-understood (best performing topic)
- ✓ No participants dengan extreme low outlier di awal test (suggests no sabotage/gaming)

### 5.3 Risk Assessment

| Risk Category | Risk Level | Evidence | Mitigation |
|---------------|-----------|----------|-----------|
| **Compliance Risk** | 🔴 HIGH | 27% below passing, Compliance staff lowest | Immediate remedial + enforcement |
| **Operational Risk** | 🟠 MEDIUM | Largest operational group below average | Simplify, contextualize, monitor |
| **Technical Risk** | 🟡 LOW | Tech staff performing above average | Maintain & deepen technical depth |
| **Executive Risk** | 🟠 MEDIUM | Wide variation in exec awareness | C-level security brief + alignment |
| **Systematic Risk** | 🟡 LOW | No systemic pattern, variance normal | Continue structured training program |

## VII. ADDITIONAL ANALYSIS: SECURITY CULTURE ASSESSMENT

### 7.1 Beyond Test Scores: Security Mindset Indicators

Berdasarkan jawaban kualitatif di post-test, berikut indikasi security mindset peserta:

#### **Positive Behavioral Indicators 🌟**
- Strong understanding bahwa incident harus dilaporkan dengan cepat (96% avg)
- Awareness tinggi tentang tidak boleh share sensitive info through personal channels (91% avg)
- Good recognition tentang data retention responsibility (96% avg)
- Deepfake & fraud detection awareness responsive (91% avg)

#### **Concerning Behavioral Indicators 🔴**
- Weak understanding tentang IP ownership & asset protection (34% avg)
- Confusion tentang when/how to classify data sensitivity (27% avg)
- Poor grasp tentang control assessment & self-evaluation (37% avg)
- Misconception tentang password policy & technical requirements (38% avg)

#### **Implication untuk Security Culture Development:**
1. **Awareness exists tapi application lemah** → Training approach: from "know that" to "know how"
2. **Regulatory/threat-based awareness tinggi** → Use fear/compliance angle dalam messaging
3. **Technical control adoption mungkin menghadapi resistance** → Need change management + simplification
4. **Gap antara knowledge dan behavior likely exists** → Need observation & behavior monitoring, bukan hanya test score

- Reward: Compliance certificate, recognition as compliant team

**Segment 2: Risk-Aware (Understanding Impact)**

**Segment 3: Career-Focused (Want to Advance)**
- Message: Security expertise as career skill, advancement opportunity
- Channel: Training certificate, skill development program, career path
- Reward: Champions program, internal consulting opportunity, salary consideration

**Segment 4: Team/Culture-Driven (Respond to Peer Influence)**
- Message: Team success, peer recognition, collective achievement
- Channel: Leaderboard, team competition, department recognition
- Reward: Team acknowledgment, team outing/event, internal social recognition

```
   - Re-test after 30 days (no record if pass)
   - Focus on development, not punishment
```

### 7.4 Department-Specific Culture Interventions

- Focus: Make security "easy" tidak "intrusive"
- Strategy: Integrate security ke existing workflows, bukan additional step

**Untuk Compliance Legal (Lowest performing)**
- Focus: Connect regulatory knowledge ke technical implementation
- Strategy: Bridge gap antara legal requirement dan control design

**Untuk Technology (Best performing)**
- Focus: Deepen expertise, involve in strategy
- Strategy: Position tech team sebagai security implementation partner

**Untuk Executive (Wide variation)**
- Focus: Ensure alignment & support
- Strategy: Executive-level security briefing & governance
